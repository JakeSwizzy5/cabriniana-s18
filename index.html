About the Collection

Cabriniana Collection, Holy Spirit Library, Cabrini University, 610 King of Prussia Rd., Radnor, PA 19087

The archival material in the Cabriniana Collection housed in the Cabriniana Room at Holy Spirit Library is open to those interested in the role of St. Frances Cabrini and the work of the Missionary Sisters of the Sacred Heart of Jesus (MSC) in the United States. The material includes letters, diaries, reports, and clippings dating from 1889. Areas covered by these materials include New York City, Newark, NJ, Philadelphia, Scranton, Chicago, New Orleans, Seattle, Denver and Los Angeles. The documentation is mostly in Italian, though some material is in English.

The archives contain duplicates of the extensive correspondence of Frances Cabrini from the General Curia of the MSCs in Rome. Basic documents of the MSCs and publications and notes on the spirituality and motivations of Cabrini's life and action are also housed in the archives. Valuable interviews with Cabrini's contemporaries are found in the duplicates of the proceedings for Cabrini's canonization.

The archives are open to scholars and other qualified visitors by appointment.
